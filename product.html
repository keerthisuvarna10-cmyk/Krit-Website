Authenticity

How to Verify Original Natural Latex

The market is full of synthetic blends sold as "latex". Here is how to identify genuine natural latex across brands.

👃
The Smell Test

Genuine natural latex has a mild, milky rubber scent that fades in 1-2 days. Synthetic latex has a sharp chemical odour that lingers. If it smells like a tyre factory — it's fake.

🌡️
The Temperature Test

Press your hand into the pillow for 10 seconds. Natural latex stays cool and springs back instantly. Memory foam retains heat and recovers slowly. Cool + instant rebound = real latex.

⚖️
The Weight Test

A genuine natural latex pillow is noticeably heavier than a synthetic one of the same size. Natural latex is dense and solid. Light and hollow feeling? It's synthetic foam pretending to be latex.

🔍
The Pin-Core Test

Unzip the cover and look inside. Genuine natural latex usually has uniform pin-core holes running through the core, created during the Dunlop or Talalay moulding process.

🔥
The Burn Test

Take a small scrap and hold a flame to it. Natural latex burns slowly, produces white/grey ash, and smells like burnt rubber. Synthetic foam catches fire, melts, drips and produces black toxic smoke.

✅
Certification & Brand Proof

Look for certification references, batch labels, traceability details, or any clear proof of latex origin. Reliable brands make those claims easy to verify.
